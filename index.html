About

I’m Matthew Plambeck, a senior at the College of William & Mary majoring in data science and minoring in history. My interests lie at the intersection of data analysis and the humanities, where I use modern statistical and computational tools to uncover insights from complex and abstract data.

Much of my current work centers on World War II records, where I build simulations, models, and visualizations to study the dynamics of convoys, logistics, and decision-making. I’m driven by the challenge of translating complex datasets into clear stories that reveal patterns, context, and human impact.

Outside of this, I enjoy golfing, traveling, and reading mystery novels.


Tools I Use: Python (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, SciPy, Scikit-Learn, PyTorch, and other libraries), R, and SQL
